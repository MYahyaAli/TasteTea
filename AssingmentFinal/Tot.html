An Insel for Tea

Sri Lanka 's central highlands have the perfect tea ecosystem to develop. The tea production was fast growing because of the plants' high weather efficiency. The remainder is the history of Ceylon Tea.
Ceylon Tea flavors come from the seven tea regions of Sri Lanka. Every one of them was perished by a distinctive appeal for gourmets that require a wide range of choices.
A cup of Ceylon Tea is only the best, lightest to toughest, full-bodied to soft.

  • Black Tea

    The processes of Black Tea allow the Polyphenoloxidase enzyme a complete rein such that the substrate- the polyphenols found in the tea leaf can be either fermented or oxidized. The black teas accompanied by green teas are among the most widely consumed. Oolong tea in Taiwan is very common. All these teas have health advantages known to them.
    Black tea is commonly more elegant than other teas.

  • Oolong Tea

    Oolong tea is Chinese tea traditional.
    The differentiation is the handling of tea.
    All tea leaves contain certain enzymes that induce a chemical reaction known as oxidation. Oxidation turns a deep dark shade to the green tea leaves.
    In contrast to the other tea Oolong tea is permitted to oxidize, so that it is partly oxidized.
    The oolong tea's color and signature flavor are responsible for this partial oxidation.
    Yet, the color of the leaves will vary from green to dark brown between various brands.

  • Green Tea

    Green Tea originated in China and also grown in Japan, Vietnam , Taiwan, India and Sri Lanka is unfermented with medium, light and at times astringent characteristics. Green Tea is also produced in China. Before drying and sifting, the leaf is picked, cleaned, heated and then rolled. Various methods represent special green tea.

  • White Tea

    White Tea is one of the popular and most refined teas of a seasonable tea drinker called Silver Tips in Sri Lanka. Although most of the world's "White Tea" tea are just a mix of green and white teas, real White Tea is a special look with a silvery leaf, a velvety feel and bright, golden liquor distilling.
    Natural white tea, silver tops, is made of the hand-picked buds of the Camellia Sinensis vine.
