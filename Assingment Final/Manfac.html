Fabricators of Tea in Sri Lanka

In 1872, the Loolecondera Estate opened a completely built tea mill.
The world premiere of ceylon tea in 1873 was when 23 pounds of James Taylor's tea reached London. Many of those in the entire hill country that visited Loolecondera in the 1880's grew rapidly in tea production in Sri Lanka, through understanding the fundamentals of tea growing and making. The Central Tea Factory in Nuwara Eliya was constructed in 1884 on the Fairyland Estate (Pedro). By 1899, almost 400,000 acres of farmland had been cultivated for tea.
The Propaganda Board of Ceylon Tea was founded in 1932.
In 1965, Sri Lanka was the world's largest tea exporter.
As a symbol of 100 percent Pure Ceylon, the Sri Lankan Tea Board internationally marked the Lion logo in 1999.
During this time major companies started their tea plantation and started exporting under the Sri Lankan Tea Board.

Akbar Brothers

Akbar Brothers has successfully diversifated into a number of industries through strategic reinvention and growth, globally recognised as one of Sri Lanka's largest tea exporter. The Akbarally family has been engaged in the collection, mixing, packaging and export of fine Ceylon tea on the mountain slopes of Sri Lanka for almost as long as it was made.
The Company currently has a significant presence in the tea, energy supply, health care, packaging, land production and environmental services industries.

GEORGE STEUART TEAS PVT LTD

George Steuart & Company is the oldest mercantile company in Sri Lanka and was founded in 1835 by brothers James and George Steuart. The ‘ Steuart Tea 'range of teas includes Black Tea, Green Tea, Herbal Tea and Flavoured Tea which make up the bulk of the export portfolio, bound for key global markets.
In 2013, George Steuart has embarked on a new journey to sell the best of pure Sri Lankan Tea to the delight of the Sri Lankan consumer.
Steuart Teas hark back to that period when the senses of British nobles were delighted with Ceylon Tea.
